Taekwondo
A martial art Dating back to the 1940's

Taekwondo origionally called Subak is a self defence martial art that was developed in the 1940's. It has many techniques that date back as far as 2000 years ago. It origionated in Korea. Due to it's defensive style it gained popularity quickly spreading to other countries.
Taekwondo can be practiced in two different ways. It can either be practiced traditionally, where you learn different blocks and kicks as you progress through the belts. The second practice is a more fighting based approach. In this style you enlist in tournaments and fight against people in your age and weight class. Both practices provide various benefits to the students and it is their choice, for how they want to train. The National Taekwondo Federation is the company that runs most national tournaments.
There are 5 main tenets that all students must follow. They are Courtesy, Integrity, Perseverance, Self-Control and Indomitable spirit. All students follow these tenets not only in the school but also outside the school. Many of these values are used everyday by a lot of people but all Taekwondo students practice these tenets everyday.
